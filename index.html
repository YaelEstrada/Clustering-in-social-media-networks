Social Network Analysis

Exploring connections and interests in high--school student social networks in the late 2000s

Project Overview

This analysis examines a bipartite social network of students and their interests, with visualizations of the network structure and key insights.

The dataset includes 11,575 students aged 14-20 with their expressed interests across 36 categories.

Key Findings:

  • The network contains 11,611 nodes (students + interests) and 35,550 edges (connections)
  • Music is the most central interest, followed by shopping and religion-related interests
  • Clear gender-based patterns emerge in interest distributions
  • Age groups show distinct preference clusters

Methodology

Data Processing:

  • Cleaned dataset of 11,575 students aged 14-20
  • Converted interests to binary values (0/1)
  • Normalized age and gender fields

Network Construction:

  • Bipartite graph with students and interests as separate node types
  • Edges represent expressed interests
  • Used NetworkX for graph analysis and Sigma.js for visualization

Analysis Techniques:

  • Degree centrality to identify influential interests
  • Community detection (Louvain method) for clustering
  • Demographic heatmaps for pattern visualization
